ID Badge

About the ID Badge

  • About: Identification badges are essential for accessing Cisco buildings and facilities.
  • Prerequisite: User ID established in the employee/contractor directory
  • Instructions:
    1. Check with the front desk to see if your User ID has been established on the directory.
    2. Once established, you will need to go to Security Room located at 163C 1st floor map) in Building 9 (see campus map) with a form of picture id (license, passport, etc.) to get your Cisco Contractor Identity Badge.
    3. If you lose or forget your badge, you will need request a temporary day-use badge from the Lobby Ambassador. Building 8 does not have an Ambassador, you will have to go to Building 9 to obtain a temporary badge.
  • More Info: Safety and Security Client Services

Laptop

Laptop

  • About: Your Manager will open a support case to request your laptop and accessories which should be available to you on your first day at Cisco, but often are not.
  • Access: Laptops - CEC
    1. Follow the instructions on the page. Contact your manager if you are unable to put in your own request.

While you are waiting for your laptop

  • If you do not have a laptop when you start, use the ID Lab computers (once we have lab access again).
  • Ask your manager to open a support case to get your computer set up

When you get your laptop

  • Don't try to log in first, it makes IT grumpy
  • Once you have your new laptop follow the instructions at New Hire Computer Setup
    • This two-day process takes you through several important steps. For steps that are starred* below, you can find more ID specific info about them further down this page.
    1. Day One
      1. Setting up passwords,
      2. Connected Backup - enables backups on your computer,
      3. Setting up a printer, [in building 8 there are 2 located behind the elevators on the second floor in the room marked 'Post Office', note the printers are multipurpose and are also copiers and fax machines]
      4. Outlook email and integrating Webex (used for sharing and dialins in meetings),
      5. Getting a phone number*
      6. and requesting your VPN access (needed so you can work remotely, it takes time to process the request)
    2. Day 2
      1. Setting up the Webex tools so they integrate with Outlook so you can invite people to meetings
      2. including Webex Connect (the instant messaging service we use)
      3. OnRamp where you request accounts for the various servers you will need access to
      4. using SoftTracker?* to download software you need that isn't preinstalled ,
      5. setting your SoftToken? which will let you use AnyConnect? so that you can use your Cisco laptop to connect to the Cisco network from home or other locations
  • More Info: For help, call Support 408-526-8888, or 800-888-8187, x6-8888
